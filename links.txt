Opt-Out Service / Disappear


California Consumer Privacy Act - CCPA
General Data Protection Regulation - GDPR

When requesting to opt-out and have your information removed from a website, observe the following:

(1) Never provide your driver's license, passport, birth certificate, or other government-issued ID.
(2) Only provide your social security number or date of birth to trusted entities, like the credit bureaus.
(3) Most of the big data brokers will opt you out by simply identifying you by your name and address.
(4) Look for links like "Do Not Sell My Information" at the bottom of a webpage, or "Opt-Out" or "Control Information" next to your data record. These should provide a means to have your data removed.
(5) Other ways to opt-out are via the website's contact page, via a support email address, or toll-free telephone number.
(6) Also, check for opt-out instructions or links in the website's privacy policy or terms of service pages.
(7) Keep in mind that even though your data was removed it may still show for a few days due to caching issues. You may have to reload the page or clear the cache in your browser. In some cases, I even had to request the website clear the search cache on their end.
(8) If the info shows in Google Search, you can request to have it removed in the Google Search Console.
